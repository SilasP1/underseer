Thistle / Distributed seismic sensing

Detect activity beyond the camera.

Thistle is a developing field-sensing system designed to detect ground-coupled activity and cue the monitoring tools operators already use.

PrototypeHardware + edge sensingEarly field testing
01 / System role

A quiet detection layer for uncovered ground.

Cameras and other high-detail systems can only respond to activity they cover or are directed toward. Thistle is being developed as a distributed, low-power cue layer: it notices ground-coupled events, associates them with place and time, and helps direct attention. It is intended to complement—not replace—higher-fidelity monitoring systems.

01

Observes

Distributed sensing nodes monitor for relevant ground-coupled activity in the area around them.

02

Reports

Detected activity becomes a compact event with the context needed for another system to act on it.

03

Supports

Events are designed to cue existing cameras, radar, DAS, VMS, or operator review workflows.

02 / How it fits

Detection first. Verification second.

The public product model is intentionally simple: notice activity, package the event, cue the right tool, and keep the operator in the decision loop.

01 / Field

Sense

Nodes watch for ground-coupled activity across a distributed field layer.

02 / Event

Localize

The system associates a detection with useful time and place context.

03 / System

Cue

An event can be handed to the monitoring equipment or software already in the workflow.

04 / Operator

Verify

Higher-detail tools and human judgment determine what the event means and what happens next.

03 / Intended environments

Built around real coverage gaps.

01

Mobile & temporary security

A deployable cue layer for places where permanent monitoring infrastructure is limited or impractical.

02

Infrastructure corridors

Additional awareness along distributed assets and other areas that are difficult to watch continuously.

03

Field research & evaluation

A platform for collecting representative data and testing where distributed ground sensing is useful.

04 / Development status

Being tested before it is promised.

Thistle is in prototype development. Current work includes bench testing, early field signal collection, architecture refinement, and learning how site conditions affect the system. Any future pilot would be scoped against a specific environment and a clear operating question.

Performance can vary with terrain, soil, installation, activity, and surrounding noise. Underseer is not publishing a final range or production-readiness claim at this stage.
